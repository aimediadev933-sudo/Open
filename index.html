Project Overview

Mission

Building upon the original OF@TEIN project to create an open, federated, SDN/NFV/Cloud-integrated testbed spanning across Asia. We enable TEIN-connected countries to collaborate on future Internet research through innovative infrastructure deployment.

Innovation

Deploying SmartX Boxes and DataLake infrastructures at academic institutions, emphasizing DevOps-style operations, capacity building, and experimentation on federated infrastructure with advanced visualization tools.

Collaboration

Ambassadors, players, and student fellows lead training, operations, and R&D efforts. GitHub, Facebook groups, and ticketing systems enhance collaborative operations across multiple timezones and countries.

Key Activities

SmartX Deployment

Deployed SmartX boxes and microboxes across over 12 countries, including new access centers and upgraded core sites to enable federated experimentation infrastructure.

DataLake Systems

Built centralized and high-availability DataLakes at GIST, UM, and NCKU to support monitoring, visualization, and DevOps-style automated testbed operations.

Training & Workshops

Conducted training on SmartX usage, ONOS, DevOps, multipath routing, SDX orchestration, and data visualization across partner institutions with over 60 participants.

Student Exchange

36 student fellows engaged in short-term exchanges and testbed experiments, promoting technical learning and cross-border collaboration in IoT/Cloud integration.

Community Building

Launched GitHub repositories, helpdesk systems, and shared automation scripts to empower partner sites with open-source resources for testbed management.

Research Groups

Formed developer groups focused on SDX fabrics, IoT/SDN, SmartX orchestration, and performance visualization, supporting real-time experimentation.

Key Outcomes

Federated Testbed

A scalable, federated testbed deployed across 12+ countries, supporting research on SDN, NFV, and Cloud networking via SmartX infrastructure and distributed architectures.

Capacity Building

Trained over 50 researchers and students in DevOps, network orchestration, and smart box deployment across regional hubs via student exchange programs.

Open Innovation

Community software and automation tools launched through GitHub repositories, empowering collaborative development and knowledge sharing across the network.
